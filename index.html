Atang Pares Overload Logo Design and Visual Identity

Atang Overload Pares is a new establish Street Food stall selling Pares. "Pares" is a popular Filipino dish that consists of beef stewed in a sweet and savory sauce, typically served with garlic fried rice (sinangag) and a bowl of clear beef broth on the side.

And seeking for approachable and accessible dine in experience on the street design. They prefer a playful yet legible font that reflects the casual and friendly atmosphere of the stall. The color palette are warm and appetizing, with hues of red, orange, and yellow to evoke feelings of passionate flavor.



I included an overflowing bowl of fonts in my sketch to represent the extra amount of beef meat in pares food products. As a Filipino, a spoon and fork are typically used when dining in. Food is served hot and freshly cooked with a flavorful smoke on both sides of the logo design.





During project collaboration with my client. My clients want to imitate the rival logo's design format, even though their brand identity differs in many ways of their competitor.

I do my best to briefly describe the significance of the competitor's logo's shapes, colors, and design. how they differ greatly from their target market and value. My explanation was accepted and well-received by my clients.


Libintia Caja Mortuary Care Logo Design and Visual Identity

Libintia Caja Mortuary Care is providing funeral Services. A decent and affordable Funeral services for Filipino Citizen.

And seeking for dignified and compassionate logo that reflects on commitment to providing respectful and comforting support to families during their time of loss. They envision a logo that conveys a sense of tranquility, solace, and reverence, while also symbolizing the eternal journey of the departed.

The color palette should be subdued and respectful, with shades of dark blue and sky blue to evoke feelings of peace and solemnity.

The white dove and wings represent peace, the ribbon provides comforting support to bereaved families, and these reasons are why I chose the emblem logo type.

My mother founded Libintia Caja in 2015. Since it is a startup, the company does not have the resources to pay a logo creator. I do offer my mother a free logo design as a way to support her business as a student with skill in digital design.

At that time, I was unfamiliar with sketching and using Adobe Illustrator for logo design, so I created this one in Adobe Photoshop. This is the first logo I designed and used for a company's visual identity.



Wedding Invitation Designs

I use Adobe InDesign and Photoshop to design the invitation. The challenges I discovered were that the contrast on digital designs is brighter, however the contrast on printed designs is darker when using photo paper.

The solution I came up with was to reduce the brightness of the screen monitor to less than 50%. The outcome is that the digital design's contrast matches the printed design.



Tarpaulin Designs

I designed the Tarpaulin using Adobe Photoshop. Designing a high dimension in Photoshop with a mid-range computer is a challenge. I employ the Upscaling process, which takes a design from low resolution to high resolution. The Preserve Details 2.0 Function is highly useful for upscalling.
